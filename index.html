Three Learning experiences

Interdisciplinary Approach to Ecological Farming

 An interdisciplinary approach incorporating principles from agriculture, economics, and social impact assessment was required to investigate how vertical farming in Pakistan may contribute to sustainable urban food production and connect with SDG 2.3.

 Reflection: I became more adept at bridging different fields of study as a result of this experience, realizing how sustainable agriculture affects society and the economy. The significance of adopting a comprehensive approach to tackle intricate problems within the realm of food security was underscored.


Analysis and Modeling of Financial Data in Agricultural Economics

 Determining if vertical farming in Pakistan's cities is economically feasible required a thorough examination of financial modeling, considering variables including startup costs, ongoing operating costs, and possible income sources.

 Reflection: This event improved my financial analysis abilities in the particular field of agriculture economics. My ability to manage intricate financial data and create scenarios enhanced my understanding of the viability of vertical farming from an economic standpoint and improved my ability to develop sustainable businesses.


Community-Based Social Impact Evaluation

 Examining how vertical farming affects local communities socially highlighted the value of a community-centered approach to social impact evaluation, emphasizing the creation of jobs, the improvement of skills, and easier access to fresh fruits and vegetables.

 Reflection: By exploring the qualitative facets of community development, this experience sharpened my abilities to create and carry out evaluations that are beyond quantitative measurements. It added to my knowledge of community-driven projects by highlighting the importance of inclusion and social well-being when assessing the success of vertical farming programs.
